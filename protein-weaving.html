Protein Weaving

Workshop Output · Emergence, February 2026

The Idea

Kagome weaving is a traditional tri-axial weaving technique from Japan that, frankly, looks really cool. Even cooler: slight changes in the pattern can change the topology of the weave — some architects have used this property to create structures that feel almost organic. We thought, hey, wouldn't it be cool to make something really organic.

We Made

  • A few simple shapes (with both flat and curving weaves) out of paper and pipe cleaners. It might evoke a grade-school project, but the math is actually pretty complicated.
  • A program that converts any closed surface to a crochet pattern and/or a weaving pattern. Thanks, Claude.
  • A crochet 1CRN (aka Crambin) protein. It's not quite as cute as a cuttlefish, but when you hold it in your hand, you can clearly see its globular structure (Charlotte tells me that's a technical term).
  • A program that visualizes a weaving pattern's surface curvature as you introduce changes in the pattern. The idea is to eventually make an animation that shows how a surface morphs to accommodate new topologies.
