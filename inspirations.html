Inspirations & Future Curation

Works, references, and ideas that preceded, sparked, and will outlast the residency.

A workshop generates more ideas than it can execute. These are the things we encountered that lodged themselves — works by others that shaped how we thought, things we wanted to make but didn't get to, and open questions we're still carrying. Consider this a living document.

Flocking Lights, Turing Patterns

One cluster of ideas we kept circling: collective behavior, emergence from simple rules, and what happens when you make that process visible — or beautiful, or physical.

Collective motion & simulation

  • Murmuration computational model ↗

    Fish schooling behavior was originally modelled by Craig Reynolds in 1986 as Boids. The same rules — separation, alignment, cohesion — produce the shapes you see in starling murmurations. The schooling behaviour of fish is also exploited on an industrial scale: purse seiner vessels use spotter planes to locate schools, then encircle them with nets.

  • Ants foraging ↗

    Individual agency building into collective macrostructure. A simulation of ant colony foraging behavior.

  • Kuramoto model ↗

    A mathematical model for synchronization — how oscillators with different natural frequencies can lock together through coupling. Applies to fireflies, neurons, power grids.

  • Sage Jenson — Physarum transport simulation ↗

    Simulation of slime mold (Physarum polycephalum) transport networks. Everything Sage Jenson is doing is probably relevant — see their portfolio PDF.

  • Everest Pipkin — Desire Paths for Wikipedia ↗

    A browser extension that tracks users' wandering paths through Wikipedia, making the invisible trails of collective curiosity visible.

Biological computation & organoids

  • Diarmid Flatley — Simulacra Naturae ↗

    A collaborative media installation exploring the entanglement of digital morphogenesis and the interplay between biological and artificial intelligences. Brain organoids drive simulations of agent-based ecosystems — flocks, slime molds, termites. Their spontaneous neural activity modulates the behavior of agents, influencing real-time generative visuals, spatial sound, sculptural forms, and living plants.

  • Diarmid Flatley — Organoid Protonoesis ↗

    A real-time interface connecting spontaneous brain organoid activity with external devices. Neuronal firing data, converted to hyperedges within hypergraphs, are transformed into computational architectural morphologies, generative spatialized sound, mechanical movements, and AI virtual entities. A brain organoid is a three-dimensional organ model produced in the lab from pluripotent stem cells — self-organized structures that mimic aspects of early brain development.

  • Collective behavior and conservation biology ↗

    How schooling and flocking behavior creates vulnerabilities that are exploited by industrial fishing — the flip side of collective intelligence.
