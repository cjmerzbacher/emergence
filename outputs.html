Artifacts

Products, as in those of a reaction. Yield, as in what's given by the ground.

A week in the Scottish countryside is certainly enough time to learn, think, read, and take the first steps toward making things. We've documented those first steps below — but first, we have an invitation.

The Catalogue

What's more delightful than a little book? Museum curators have known this for decades — that's why they package images and ideas from their exhibits into catalogs you can pick up in the gift shop. We're doing the same thing: collecting finished pieces, unfinished pieces, loose thoughts, ambitious ideas, rants/raves, reviews, revues, cool rocks, and insights into something we can share. We'd like you to contribute.

Call for Submissions

We welcome contributions in any form that engages with the themes of the residency: emergence, complexity, the relationship between parts and wholes, generativity, and the conditions under which something new comes into being.

Example submissions

  • Short essay or critical writing
  • Fiction, poetry, or other literary work
  • Artwork, photographs, or visual documentation
  • Musical score, audio notation, or written description of a musical work
  • Technical or scientific writing

Guidelines

  • You will retain all rights to your work.
  • Contributions will be edited (in conversation with you).
  • Images should be submitted at 300 dpi or higher.
  • If your work is primarily digital, please also submit something we can put in print.

Deadline: 1 May 2026

Submit via email to cjmerzbacher at gmail dot com

The catalogue will live both online and in print. The print edition will be limited run, so please reach out if you want to reserve a copy.
